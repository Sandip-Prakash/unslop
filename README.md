# Unslop
### The anti-slop content framework for AI agents

> Taste Skill fixed how AI-built interfaces look.
> This fixes how AI-written words feel.

---

## Why this exists

Writing has always been the one skill I trusted most. Long before AI, people came to me for speeches, copy, difficult emails. Anything that needed the right words.

Then AI arrived.

Like everyone else, I was amazed. But after the excitement faded, something kept bothering me. I could almost instantly tell when a piece of writing had been generated by AI. The strange part was, I could not explain why.

That question became an obsession. What is my brain detecting? What makes one paragraph feel alive and another feel manufactured, even when both are grammatically perfect?

I have always loved words. Loved them enough to make terrible puns with them, which my colleagues will confirm. And loved them enough to spend years studying what makes some arrangements of them feel alive and others feel dead.

The answer was not in grammar or vocabulary. It was in psychology. Over years of copywriting, and market research where we spoke to more than 2,500 people in person, not over email or a Google Form but face to face, watching how they react, what they trust, what makes them pull back, I started seeing patterns. I studied human behavior and psychology, not academically but practically: what makes people believe something, what makes them suspicious, and most importantly, what makes them feel a real person is behind the words even when they cannot explain why. Because most of the time, the brain does that work before language catches up. We feel it before we can say it.

I organized those patterns into a framework. Not to help people beat AI detectors, but to help AI and writers produce work that carries genuine human presence.

I believe that is becoming one of the most valuable skills of this decade. As AI makes content abundant, what becomes scarce is not information. It is authenticity. The writing that stands out will be the writing that feels like a real person was behind it. That is the problem this framework was built to solve.

Unslop is that framework. Five psychological signals that reveal whether writing has a real human behind it, or just the performance of one.

## The mental model

AI now produces infinite content. The bottleneck is no longer generating. It is knowing what is good.

Here is the question most people cannot answer: why does something feel like AI? They say "it feels off" and shrug. The feeling is real. The diagnosis is missing.

The diagnosis starts with two questions you are actually asking simultaneously when you read anything:

**How much is here?** Too much signals compensation. The right amount signals confidence.

**How specific is it?** General language could have been written by anyone, about anything, from nowhere. Specific language could only have come from someone who was actually there.

Map those two questions:

```
              GENERAL
                 |
  TOO MUCH ------+------ RIGHT AMOUNT
                 |
             SPECIFIC
```

AI defaults to the top-left corner every time: too much, too general. That is the absence zone. The presence zone is bottom-right: the right amount, specific.

The five signals are the five most common ways writing lands in the wrong quadrant, and the two ways it earns the right one. The map produces the signals. They are not a list someone chose. They fall out of the structure of the problem.

---

## The five signals

**Three signals of absence** (different ways of being too much, too general):

**Signal 1: Over-effort breaks trust.**
Stacked claims, adjectives piled on adjectives, sentences that exist to impress. The brain reads: if they are working this hard to convince me, they must have something to hide. Empty vessels make the most noise. Desperation signals a lack.

**Signal 2: Over-perfection breaks connection.**
One smooth, even, rehearsed pass. No hesitation, no self-correction, no texture. The brain reads: no one was actually here working this out. We connect to the human behind the work, not the work itself. A machine's flawless output has no human to connect to. A person's honest, slightly imperfect effort does. (This is why Beeple's "Everydays: 5000 Days" sold for $69 million. The value was not the images. It was 13 years of a real person showing up every day.)

**Signal 3: Over-clutter breaks worth.**
Everything covered, every feature listed, every use case addressed. The brain reads: no real point of view, no confidence. Premium things say one thing. Cheap things shout everything. What you do not say signals as much as what you do.

**Two signals of presence** (ways of earning the right quadrant):

**Signal 4: Specificity signals lived truth.**
Real memory stores sensory detail. When someone actually lived something, the specifics are there to retrieve: the LIC trail, the fog in the valley at 8am, the bitter finish of the filter coffee at Sunrise Cafe. Category language proves absence. Detail proves presence.

**Signal 5: Narrowing signals confidence.**
Claiming to be for everyone is what insecure things do. A thing that knows exactly who it is for, and implicitly who it is not, reads as real and confident. The exclusion is the honest edge. Narrowing is not an apology. It is a declaration.

---

## The underlying law

**Excess signals hidden absence.**
**Specificity and calibrated imperfection signal real presence.**

Taste is calibrating to the halfway point: invest enough to genuinely reach the other person, never so much that it reads as compensation. Think of telling a friend about something at a wedding. Specific, unforced, no pitch. That register is the target.

## How to use it

**As a generation tool** (steer what you are about to write):

Paste the SKILL.md into your AI before writing anything. The rules load into context and apply automatically to whatever the AI generates next.

**As a curation tool** (evaluate what you have):

Paste the SKILL.md into your AI, then paste in any piece of writing and say: "Run the Presence Test on this. Score it against all five signals, pass or fail with evidence, then tell me the single highest-leverage fix."

**As a judgment tool** (your own eye):

Use the two questions as a quick scan. How much is here? How specific is it? If the answer is "too much" and "not very," you are in the absence zone. You know what to fix without running the full test.

## Install

**Option 1: Paste into any AI**
Open the SKILL.md file, copy the contents, paste at the start of your conversation. Works with Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or any LLM.

**Option 2: Add to your project**
Copy SKILL.md into your project root. Any AI agent that reads skill files will pick it up automatically.

## Before/after

**Signal 1: Over-effort**

Slop:
> Our revolutionary platform seamlessly empowers teams to unlock their full potential with cutting-edge, industry-leading tools designed to transform the way you work.

Presence:
> No more "does 3pm work? okay how about Thursday?" for the tenth time. Books the meeting in one message. You get your mornings back.

**Signal 4: Specificity**

Slop:
> I am passionate about outdoor activities and enjoy exploring new cuisines and cultures in my free time.

Presence:
> I hike the LIC trail most mornings. There is a bend where the fog sits in the valley till about 8. On the way back there is this underrated place called Sunrise Cafe, filter coffee, the bitter kind. The climb is excruciating at the start and honestly godly once you are sweating at the top. I take the long route on purpose. It makes my day.

**Signal 5: Narrowing**

Slop:
> Our platform is the perfect solution for teams of any size, offering everything you need to succeed.

Presence:
> Built for engineering teams under 20. If that is you, it fits like it was made for you, because it was.

## What to ban before anything else

Remove these first. They collapse trust before a reader reaches your message.

**Words that assert value without demonstrating it:**
Revolutionary, transformative, game-changer, seamless, powerful, cutting-edge, innovative, unleash, supercharge, elevate, unlock potential, world-class, paradigm shift, robust, synergy, dynamic, disruptive, impactful, next-level.

Replace each one with the specific true thing it was trying to gesture at.

**Punctuation:**
Em-dash and en-dash. Anywhere in the output. They are the single most common LLM punctuation tell. Use a plain hyphen, a comma, or restructure.

**Openers:**
"Certainly!", "Absolutely!", "Great question!", "I would be happy to help." Start with the answer.

---

## What this is not

Not a theory of taste. One focused lens: presence vs absence.

Not a style guide. It does not tell you what voice to have. It removes the absence of one.

Not a replacement for good thinking. It cannot make a weak idea feel strong. It makes a true thing feel as true as it is.

Not competing with visual frameworks. Taste Skill fixed how AI interfaces look. This is the verbal layer underneath.

---

## Scope

For: landing page copy, marketing emails, personal profiles, product descriptions, brand voice, social copy, any persuasive or identity writing.

Not for: legal copy, technical documentation, internal ops writing, fiction.

When in doubt, apply Signal 4 first. Specificity is the fastest fix and the least risky in any context.
