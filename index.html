Two questions reveal everything about a piece of writing. How much is here? And how specific is it? AI defaults to too much, too general. Every time. The five signals map exactly where it goes wrong, and how to fix it.

Absence has
five disguises.

05 signals
01
Absence

Over-effort breaks trust

Stacked claims, adjectives piled on adjectives, sentences that exist to impress. The brain reads: if they are working this hard to convince me, they must have something to hide. Empty vessels make the most noise.

02
Absence

Over-perfection breaks connection

One smooth, even, rehearsed pass. No hesitation, no texture. We connect to the human behind the work, not the work itself. A flawless surface has no human to connect to.

03
Absence

Over-clutter breaks worth

Everything covered, every feature listed. Premium things say one thing. Cheap things shout everything. What you do not say signals as much as what you do.

04
Presence

Specificity signals lived truth

Real memory stores sensory detail. When someone actually lived something, the specifics are there. Category language proves absence. Detail proves presence.

05
Presence

Narrowing signals confidence

Claiming to be for everyone is what insecure things do. A thing that knows exactly who it is for reads as real and confident. Narrowing is not an apology. It is a declaration.

The replacement rule

Replace every banned word with the specific true thing it was trying to gesture at. Not "revolutionary platform" but what it actually does. Not "seamless experience" but what the person actually feels. The specific true thing beats any adjective.

This will not change your tone of voice. Think of it as an expert copywriter sitting next to you who knows your voice, catches when you drift into slop, and pulls you back without rewriting who you are. It preserves the tone. It strips the AI residue.

Why this exists

"I could tell when writing was AI-generated.
The strange part was, I could not explain why."

Over years of copywriting and market research, conversations with more than 2,500 people in person, face to face, certain patterns kept appearing. Human writing carries signals that are hard to fake: lived experience, emotional risk, specific observation, genuine intent. Unslop is those patterns, organized into something anyone can use.
